# The Tail You Cannot Fit — Project Instructions

## 1. Project Overview

- **Short Description:** A Peaks-Over-Threshold extreme value analysis of NexaCore Financial Technologies' operational loss history, producing GPD-fitted VaR and Expected Shortfall estimates at the 99th, 99.9th, and 99.95th percentiles and quantifying the capital model error introduced by a log-normal distributional assumption.

- **Description:** This project applies Extreme Value Theory (EVT) to NexaCore's synthetic operational loss register to demonstrate a structural deficiency in conventional distributional assumptions. The central argument is that operational losses — clustered in events that are rare, severe, and structurally dissimilar to the bulk of the loss distribution — violate the conditions under which log-normal or normal-distribution-based VaR is asymptotically valid. Using the Peaks-Over-Threshold (POT) method, the project fits a Generalized Pareto Distribution (GPD) to exceedances above a calibrated threshold and produces tail risk estimates that are directly compared against log-normal equivalents fitted to sub-threshold ("bulk") losses, and against historical simulation. The capital buffer gap between the models is the operative finding. The document is written for CROs and capital adequacy committees operating under Basel III / CRD V.

- **Abstract:** *(~200 words, draft)*

  > Operational risk capital models built on log-normal loss assumptions produce estimates that are biased precisely at the quantiles that determine regulatory capital adequacy. This project applies the Peaks-Over-Threshold method from Extreme Value Theory to NexaCore Financial Technologies' operational loss register, fitting a Generalized Pareto Distribution to losses exceeding a threshold calibrated via mean excess and shape-stability diagnostics. The analysis produces Value-at-Risk and Expected Shortfall estimates at the 99th, 99.9th, and 99.95th percentiles and compares them against a log-normal benchmark fitted to sub-threshold losses — representing the model a practitioner without an EVT framework would plausibly build — and against historical simulation. The GPD estimates a positive shape parameter, confirming heavy-tail behavior; at every confidence level tested, GPD-derived capital requirements exceed the log-normal estimate by a material margin, while historical simulation's tail estimate flattens at the empirical maximum loss once required quantiles exceed the available sample's extremes. For institutions operating under Basel III Advanced Measurement Approaches or CRD V internal model provisions, the choice of loss distribution is a capital adequacy decision with board-level implications, not a technical parameter selection.

- **Target audience:** CROs, capital adequacy committees, model risk officers, board risk committees in EU financial institutions.
- **Output format:** Self-contained HTML (`embed-resources: true`)
- **Render command:** `quarto render index.qmd`
- **Expected output path:** `output/index.html`

- **Data sources:**
  - `data/loss_register.csv` — Synthetic operational loss event register; 500 rows; generated by `generate_losses.py` with `random.seed(42)`. Two-component mixture: 85% log-normal bulk, 15% Pareto tail. Fields: `event_date`, `business_line`, `basel_event_type`, `gross_loss_eur`, `recovery_eur`, `net_loss_eur`, `component` (validation-only, not used in document).
  - `data/threshold_diagnostics.csv` — Mean excess plot values across 87 candidate thresholds; generated by `generate_losses.py`.
  - `data/gpd_fit.csv` — GPD parameter estimates (ξ, σ), standard errors, GEV cross-check parameters; generated by `compute_evt.R`.
  - `data/capital_comparison.csv` — VaR and ES at 99%, 99.9%, 99.95% under GPD, log-normal (sub-threshold fit), and historical simulation, with euro and percentage gaps; generated by `compute_evt.R`.
  - `data/qq_pp_data.csv` — Empirical and GPD-theoretical quantiles/probabilities for the probability and QQ diagnostic plots; generated by `compute_evt.R`.
  - `data/return_level_data.csv` — Return level estimates at 9 return periods (2–1000 events); generated by `compute_evt.R`.
  - `data/stability_results.csv` — Shape parameter ξ refit across a grid of candidate thresholds; generated by `compute_evt.R`.
  - `data/annual_maxima.csv` — Annual maximum net loss by year (2020–2024), used for GEV cross-check; generated by `compute_evt.R`.

- Basel III (not Basel 3); CRD V (not CRD5)
- "Sub-threshold log-normal benchmark" — the specific term for the log-normal model in this document; do not refer to it simply as "the log-normal model" without qualification, since it is fit to bulk losses only, not the full register

Number formatting: monetary values in €X,XXX format. Percentages one decimal place in body text. Confidence levels as "99th percentile" in prose, "99%" in tables.
